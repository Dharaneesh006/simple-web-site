Batman, created by artist Bob Kane and writer Bill Finger, first appeared in Detective Comics #27 in 1939. His true identity is Bruce Wayne, a wealthy American playboy, philanthropist, and industrialist residing in Gotham City. As a child, Bruce witnessed the murder of his parents, Thomas and Martha, which fueled his unwavering vendetta against criminals. Batman’s origin story intertwines vengeance with the ideal of justice. He trains himself physically and intellectually, adopting a bat-inspired persona to patrol Gotham’s streets at night. Unlike most superheroes, Batman lacks superpowers, relying instead on intellect, combat skills, and wealth.
